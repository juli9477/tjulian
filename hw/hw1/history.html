Fluxus Movement

Interactive Design was greatly shaped by the Fluxus movement, which centers around a self sufficient aesthetic, anti-commercialism and anti-art sensibility. The Fluxus movement is not a modern-art movement or style, instead it is seen as an international organization which includes artists from around the world. There are twelve essential ideas that make up Fluxus, these include: Globalism, Unity of Art and Life, Intermedia, Experimentalism, Chance, Playfulness, Simplicity, Implicativeness, Exemplativism, Specificity, Presence in time and Musicality.

Internet and Interactive Design

With the rise of internet usage, interactive media and computing over time fused together to make digital interactive consumer products. The cultures of design and engineering, created a common goal in flexible use and user experience.

Interactive vs Interaction

Many times people confuse Interactive Design with Interaction. Interactive Design includes the building of a meaningful uses of hardware and systems, while Interaction Design is the design of those hardware and systems. Both of these designs are essential to each other.
